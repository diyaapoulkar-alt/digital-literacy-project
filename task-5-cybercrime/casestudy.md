# UPI / Online Payment Fraud Case Study

UPI fraud is a common type of cybercrime in which attackers trick users into sending money or revealing sensitive financial information. This often happens through fake payment requests, phishing links, or impersonation.

In a typical scenario, a student receives a call or message from someone pretending to be a bank official or a buyer/seller. The attacker may send a UPI “collect request” and ask the user to approve it, claiming that money will be received. In reality, approving the request results in money being debited from the user’s account.

In another case, users are sent fake links asking them to enter UPI PIN or banking details. Once entered, the attacker gains access to the account and transfers money without permission.

Students and young users are often targeted due to lack of awareness. The consequences include financial loss, stress, and misuse of personal banking information.

This case highlights the importance of understanding how UPI transactions work and being cautious while handling online payments.
